Addendum A

Path to the 99-year lease

A step-by-step guide to securing long-term lease security on Agua Caliente tribal trust land — the structural foundation of this transaction.

Recommended Path (Summary)

01
Obtain a Title Status Report (TSR) from BIA Palm Springs Agency to confirm land status (tribal trust vs. allotted trust), identify the landowner(s), and surface any encumbrances or title defects.
02
Determine governing framework: whether the Agua Caliente has approved HEARTH Act regulations for commercial leasing or if the standard BIA 25 CFR Part 162 (Subpart D — Business Leases) approval path applies.
03
Engage tribal realty office (Agua Caliente Trust Services) and BIA Palm Springs Agency early — request a Preliminary Review of proposed lease terms under §162.440 before formal submission.
04
Negotiate new 99-year lease terms with the individual allottee landowner or tribal authority. This is a new lease (not an extension) — the cleanest path. The existing ~27-year lease has already been extended once and may not be eligible for further extension.
05
Secure Indian landowner consent and tribal authorization (if tribal land). Prepare the full lease package per 25 CFR §162.438: executed lease, valuation/appraisal, NEPA documentation, entity documentation, insurance, performance bond, and environmental/archaeological assessments.
06
BIA reviews the complete package within 30 days of receipt for business leases (§162.440). If approved, the lease is recorded at the Land Title and Records Office (LTRO). If incomplete, BIA notifies parties of missing items.
07
Assignment strategy: recommend the seller (Harry) execute a concurrent assignment of the existing leasehold to our GP entity, while our GP entity simultaneously applies as the new leaseholder for the 99-year term. This avoids a gap in operational control.
08
NEPA compliance: initiate environmental assessment early (Month 1–2). BIA may adopt an existing EA/EIS under 43 CFR 46.320 if prior environmental work has been completed on the parcel. Hotel use is a continuation of existing use, which simplifies NEPA.
09
Valuation: BIA requires fair market rent determination. Engage an MAI appraiser with BIA trust land experience. Current ground lease terms (5% room revenue + 1.5% other revenue) should be validated against comparable leases in the Agua Caliente portfolio.
10
Lender coordination: SBA/conventional lenders require an executed, recorded lease with a remaining term exceeding the loan term by a meaningful margin. A 99-year lease satisfies all institutional lending requirements. Target: BIA approval and LTRO recording before refi application (2029).

When do we start the 99-year lease process?

Understanding the Deal Structure

This is a building lease with purchase option — not a straight purchase. Harry remains the ground leaseholder and the borrower of record on the existing bank loan. LBHG takes operational control via an Interim Management Agreement (IMA) that converts into a building lease once Stage A approvals are obtained. During the building lease phase (3 years + 1-year extension), LBHG operates the hotel, services Harry's debt, pays ground rent, and executes the $3.75M renovation.

The purchase option is exercised when LBHG is ready to take title — which requires the 99-year ground lease to be in place and financing (SBA refi) to be arranged. Harry receives $1M upfront at lease commencement (credited against the $8M purchase price), and the remaining $7M when the purchase option is exercised. No interest accrues on the $7M balance during the building lease — which is the core economic advantage of this structure.

Can we get the 99-year lease before we own the hotel?

Not directly. Under 25 CFR Part 162, the applicant for a new ground lease must have a legitimate basis to be the leaseholder. Since Harry holds the existing ground lease and you're operating under a building lease, the BIA would need to see either: (a) a simultaneous assignment of the existing leasehold to your entity plus application for a new 99-year term, or (b) Harry cooperating as the existing leaseholder to facilitate a new lease in your name. Either way, this is most cleanly done in conjunction with the purchase option exercise — not as a standalone action during the building lease period.

RECOMMENDATION: Two-Phase Approach

Phase 1: Groundwork (Years 1–2)

During the building lease period, you can and should do the preparatory work:

— Get the TSR from BIA (understand exactly what you're dealing with)

— Identify the landowner and build the relationship

— Commission the environmental assessment (NEPA)

— Get the MAI appraisal for rent reasonableness

— Pre-negotiate lease terms with the landowner

— Request a BIA Preliminary Review of proposed terms

None of this requires you to hold the ground lease. This is intelligence gathering and relationship building that dramatically de-risks the formal application later.

Phase 2: Formal Application (Year 3)

Begin the formal 99-year lease application ~12 months before your target refi date:

— Exercise the purchase option (or apply concurrently)

— Harry assigns existing ground lease to your entity

— Submit full lease package to BIA (all prep work done in Phase 1)

— BIA approves within 30 days (complete business lease package)

— LTRO records the new 99-year lease

— Apply for SBA/conventional refi with recorded lease in hand

Because all the groundwork is done, the formal application should take 3–4 months, not 9–12. You've already done the hard work.

Why this works: Harry gets $1M cash upfront, then you operate under the building lease for 3 years with zero interest on the remaining $7M — that's the golden window. You renovate, stabilize NOI, prove the numbers. In Year 3, you exercise the purchase option, simultaneously close on the 99-year lease (which you've been pre-cooking since Year 1), and immediately apply for the SBA refi. The refi proceeds pay Harry his remaining $7M. The 99-year lease is recorded. Everyone is made whole.

Extension vs. New Lease

Option A: Extend Existing Lease

Per 25 CFR §162, leases may be extended such that the total term does not exceed 99 years. However, the existing lease has already been extended once — and per the call transcript (Jan 2026), further extension is not possible through the standard path. The BIA contact confirmed that a new lease would be required to reach 99 years.

⚠ Not recommended — prior extension exhausted

Option B: New 99-Year Lease (Recommended)

Negotiate a new ground lease for up to 99 years directly with the Indian landowner, with BIA approval. This is the cleanest path: it produces a fresh lease that satisfies all institutional lending requirements, eliminates leasehold discount on exit valuation, and provides maximum term security for investors.

✓ Recommended — maximum financeability
